Force Analysis of a Multi-Finger Robotic Gripper

Undergraduate Research Thesis

September 2019 - March 2020

Paper Code: BPA-4S-15
Organization: UGC Journal, India

This project involved the study of Jamia Hand, which is an underactuated tendon driven 4-fingered robotic gripper. An analogy is presented between the human hand and the Jamia Hand w.r.t angle of rotation, dof, number of joints, and the number of fingers. A mathematical mode is shown and the friction losses between the tendon cables and hand’s surface are calculated using the simplified Dahl model and belt pulley approach. Contact forces are then calculated, and kinematic models are shown for the motion of thumb and fingers to trace their motion at any point.
